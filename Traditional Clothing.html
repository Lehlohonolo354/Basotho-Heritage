Traditional Clothing

Basotho Traditional Clothes that make you look like royalty

Basotho Clothing

  • SESHOESHOE

    • A fabric used to make clothes that are mostly used for celebrations and funerals whether its a wedding ,graduations or anything that involves large sum of people gathering

  • MOSE OA KHOMO

    • An acient traditional dress worn by married or elderly women

  • LEBOLLO

    • A red coloured blanket,mostly worn by men who are from initiation

  • MOKOROTLO

    • A hat that is well known in basotho tradition it differentiates basotho from other tribes

  • THETHANA

    • This is worn by girls who are not yet married or those who are maidens

  • KOBO EA MOKHOHLANE

    • A blanket that's meant for funeral it is worn to show that we are in a tragic moment

  • SEFATLA

    • An acient shoe worn by our ancestors it was made with an animal skin they are rare to find this days

  • TS'ETS'E

    • This hat is mostly worn by woman its a basicaly a sun hat

  • MOSE OA LITOLOBONYA

    • A dress particularly made for young girls and women when they are about to do their cerimonial dance/entatainment.

  • PHAHLO TSA MOHOBELO

    • This are clothes worn by man when they are about to do their ceremonial dance

  • LEHLOSI

    • a blanket specifically mande and worn by a KING or those of royalty

  • PHAHLO TSA NTLAMU

    • A boys and men's apparel worn for ntlamu dance

  • SEANAMARENA

    • respectful Basotho blanket made for special events,worn as professional attire, designed also for cold days to keep you warm

  • MOLIEA NYEOE

    • A hat worn by men most especially when they go to the chief's order for any unsolved arguments

  • LEFITORI

    • A blanket named Lefitori it was named after Queen Victoria Of England since she is the one who gave the those blankets as gifts to our king

  • TS'EA

    • One of the clothes worn by young boys as they are about to enter adulthood

  • LELAKABE

    • Basotho blanket worn by men

  • LIPHOPLOANA

    • Traditional accessories such as jewelry worn to complement the attire

  • SEANAMARENA SE SETSO

    • A traditional blanket

  • SEROPE SA MOTSOETSE

    • A blanket worn by mothers who have just given birth
